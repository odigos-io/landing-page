We are excited to announce that as of v1.25 the Odigos instrumentation control
plane has added integrated support for [OpenTelemetry eBPF Instrumentation
("OBI")](https://opentelemetry.io/docs/zero-code/obi/).

This addition to our platform brings more instrumentation options (including
support for languages like C++ and Rust) and even greater compatibility with the
OpenTelemetry ecosystem to Odigos users. With this, Odigos users can now
seamlessly deploy OBI instrumentation through Odigos for any supported
applications.

While OBI is planning for its first stable release [later this
year](https://opentelemetry.io/blog/2026/obi-goals/), we are offering it through
Odigos as an opt-in tech preview. Starting today, you can choose OBI as an
instrumentation distro for any application in Odigos.

See our [documentation](https://docs.odigos.io/oss/instrumentations/obi) to
learn how to enable it for your applications.

## Why OBI?

We believe that eBPF-based auto-instrumentation is a powerful option both for
teams who are just starting to onboard with OpenTelemetry and for experienced
organizations who are building reliable, long-term solutions. For many
enterprises already, eBPF is not just a stopgap but rather the core of their
observability strategy. And for those just starting their observability journey,
eBPF offers unrivaled coverage with ease.

The addition of OBI to Odigos enhances our ability to support both ends of this
spectrum. This is because OBI integration adds a different approach to
instrumentation in our platform.

Existing instrumentations in Odigos are built on eBPF uprobes, which attach to
memory locations in user space applications and offer deep, library-level access
(such as function arguments, return values, and method receivers) for rich
telemetry. In contrast, OBI is built on socket probes that attach at the network
layer and map incoming and outgoing connections to individual requests. While
these network probes may lack the full depth of userspace instrumentation, they
offer language agnostic breadth of coverage that isn't bound to specific
libraries or runtimes.

Our existing uprobe instrumentation options aren't going anywhere, and we are
continuing to build on uprobe instrumentation as the core of our platform. But
blending these two approaches presents new possibilities for distributed tracing
coverage, exactly how you want it to look. This combination isn't available
anywhere else.

Variety lowers the barriers to access for everyone by giving end users the power
of choice and the flexibility to unlock the configuration that works best for
their needs. With OBI's community backing promising confidence in the project,
we are excited to support it as a production-ready option for our customers.

## Odigos and OBI

Bringing OBI support to Odigos required several upstream changes to the OBI
project contributed by our team. These contributions expand the capabilities of
OBI to make it more consumable by other projects including Odigos, enabling more
possibilities for vendor support of OBI.

Among these were the addition of [PID-based
discovery](https://github.com/open-telemetry/opentelemetry-ebpf-instrumentation/pull/1321)
and [dynamic application
selection](https://github.com/open-telemetry/opentelemetry-ebpf-instrumentation/pull/1388),
which is used by the Odiglet to support our operator-based configuration layer.
Prior to this, OBI relied on a static config applied at startup to select
applications to instrument. Dynamic selection allows OBI to instrument new
applications without having to reload the entire config and offers fine-grained
control over which applications to instrument by PID. This change was key to
enabling OBI management through Odigos, and we hope it will enable other
developers to build similar controllers based on OBI as well.

These changes are just a few of the many we have planned to contribute to OBI as
part of our support for the project.

## Our Commitment to Open Source

Odigos is one of several maintainers of the OBI project, and as OpenTelemetry
contributors the Odigos engineering team proudly supports the development and
advancement of a community maintained tool for users to leverage vendor-agnostic
eBPF instrumentation.

Odigos has a long history of open source contributions in the OpenTelemetry
community, starting with our [donation of the opentelemetry-go-instrumentation
project in
2022](https://github.com/open-telemetry/opentelemetry-go-instrumentation/issues/2).
That project, along with upstream collaboration from multiple other companies
including Grafana and Splunk, developed key innovations that would eventually
evolve into core aspects of OBI today.

Keeping with this effort, we plan to continue and expand our contributions to
OpenTelemetry and OBI in the future to add more features in Odigos and for the
community. Stay tuned for more updates and join us in the [OBI community
SIG](https://github.com/open-telemetry/community#specification-sigs) to get
involved!
